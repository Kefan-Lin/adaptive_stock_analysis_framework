# Industry Structure

## Objective

Map the external economics around the business before discussing moat or valuation.

## Required Sections

Always cover:
1. Value chain position
2. Supply and demand structure
3. Industry rivalry and bargaining power
4. Regulation, cycle, or technology disruption
5. Variable tree

## Value Chain Mapping

State:
- Upstream dependencies
- Core production or service bottleneck
- Distribution path
- End customer and decision maker

If the company is not the bottleneck in the chain, lower pricing-power confidence.

## Industry Forces

Score each `Low / Medium / High`:
- Rivalry
- Buyer power
- Supplier power
- Substitution risk
- Entry risk

Then state whether the current environment is:
- Structural
- Cyclical
- Policy-driven
- Transitional

## Variable Tree

Build one tree with:
- Level 1 drivers: price, volume, mix, cost, capital intensity, valuation multiple
- Level 2 drivers: sector-specific KPIs
- Visibility drivers: contract duration, renewal/retention, backlog quality, regulated
  return, lease length, reimbursement durability, or other evidence that changes
  earnings volatility and risk premium

Decision rule:
- If the thesis depends mainly on fast variables, widen valuation bands.
- If the thesis depends mainly on durable structural advantages, confidence can be higher.
- If new evidence shifts the business from spot/cyclical/project revenue toward durable
  contracted or recurring cash flow, run the Structural Re-rating Gate in
  `valuation-scenarios.md`.

## Required Output Block

Include:
1. Industry classification
2. Primary and secondary drivers
3. Three key failure triggers
4. Invariants vs variables
