# Industry Playbooks (Supplemental Overlay)

## Objective

Provide an optional structural overlay after the controller has already selected an industry skill.
Use this file to sharpen framing, not to replace the primary route to industry skills.

## Use Order

1. Route to one industry skill first.
2. Then use one structural archetype from this file if it helps sharpen the thesis.
3. If this file conflicts with the industry skill, the industry skill wins.

## Archetype Overlay

| Archetype | Typical sectors | Core question | Primary valuation | Secondary valuation |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Durable Compounder | Software infra, mission-critical platforms | Can high ROIC persist while growth decelerates? | DCF (FCFF) | P/E or EV/EBIT |
| Platform Retail | E-commerce / marketplace | Is scale translating into durable take-rate + cash conversion? | DCF / FCFE | EV/EBIT or P/E |
| Cyclical Asset Producer | Mining, materials, power generation | Where are we in the cycle and what is mid-cycle earning power? | Mid-cycle DCF | EV/EBITDA / EV-Resource |
| Capex-Heavy Turnaround | Semis/foundry/legacy infra | Is incremental capital earning above cost of capital? | Through-cycle DCF | EV/Sales or EV/EBITDA bridge |
| Pre-profit Growth Infra | AI infra, hardware-scaleout, capex-heavy growth | Can growth outrun dilution and capex burden to reach self-funded scale? | Risk-adjusted DCF | EV/Sales with margin bridge |
| Clinical Biotech | Drug development | What is probability-weighted asset value after cash burn and dilution? | rNPV | Cash-adjusted EV floor |
| Micro-cap Explorer | Early resource explorers | Is downside protected by balance sheet/asset floor before catalyst? | Asset/NAV/rNPV | EV/resource or liquidation floor |

## When It Adds Value

Use the overlay when the industry route is still too broad:
- A software company behaves like a durable compounder, not generic SaaS
- A semiconductor name is a capex-heavy turnaround, not simply “semi”
- A resource name is really a micro-cap explorer with financing risk

## Required Deliverables by Archetype

### 1) Durable Compounder
- Drivers: NRR/renewal, pricing power, OCF/FCF conversion.
- Moat attacks: platform substitution, open-source commoditization, regulation.
- Must include: reinvestment efficiency and AI/capex return path.

### 2) Platform Retail
- Drivers: GMV growth, take rate, fulfillment cost ratio, ad monetization.
- Must include: subsidy/price-war stress test and customer acquisition payback.

### 3) Cyclical Asset Producer
- Drivers: realized commodity/power price, unit cost curve position, reserve life.
- Must include: cycle-positioning table (`trough/base/peak`) and mid-cycle normalized margin.

### 4) Capex-Heavy Turnaround
- Drivers: utilization/yield, product mix, capex intensity, depreciation drag.
- Must include: ROIC bridge (pre-turnaround vs post-turnaround) and execution milestones.

### 5) Pre-profit Growth Infra
- Drivers: backlog/ARR growth, gross margin trajectory, cash burn runway, financing terms.
- Must include: dilution path and “self-funding year” assumption.

### 6) Clinical Biotech
- Drivers: phase success probability, TAM, peak sales, partner economics, cash runway.
- Must include: catalyst calendar and per-catalyst valuation jump/downside map.

### 7) Micro-cap Explorer
- Drivers: resource quality/grade, jurisdiction, infrastructure adjacency, funding runway.
- Must include: `asset floor vs option value` split and worst-case financing path.

## Invariant vs Variable Framework

For every report, separate:

- Invariants (slow-changing): product necessity, cost curve position, regulatory moat, balance-sheet resilience.
- Variables (fast-changing): commodity prices, quarterly margins, policy news, sentiment multiple.

Decision rule:
- If thesis relies mostly on variables, lower confidence and widen valuation band.
- If thesis is anchored by invariants plus clear catalysts, confidence can be higher.

## Cross-Industry Red Flags

1. Revenue growth with weak cash conversion for multiple periods.
2. “Adjusted” profits diverging from GAAP with no convergence trend.
3. High capex without evidence of improving incremental returns.
4. Dilution that offsets operating progress on per-share basis.
5. Valuation dependent on terminal assumptions that exceed industry logic.
