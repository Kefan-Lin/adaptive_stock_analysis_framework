# Business and Moat Rubric

## Objective

Decompose the company into economic drivers, identify moat sources, and judge durability under realistic competitive pressure.

## 1) Business Engine Mapping

Capture these six elements before discussing valuation:

| Element | Questions to answer | Evidence examples |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Customer job | What problem is solved? Is it mission-critical? | Retention, usage depth, contract terms |
| Demand driver | Why does demand grow or shrink? | Volume trends, penetration, replacement cycle |
| Pricing model | How does the company charge and raise price? | Price/mix history, renewal terms |
| Cost architecture | Which costs are fixed vs variable? | Gross margin bridge, SG&A leverage |
| Unit economics | Is incremental business attractive? | Contribution margin, payback, LTV/CAC |
| Reinvestment runway | Where can capital compound next? | TAM expansion, adjacent products, geography |

If unit economics are unclear, reduce confidence regardless of headline growth.

## 2) Industry Structure (Practical Five Forces)

Evaluate force strength as `Low/Medium/High` and describe trend over the next 3 years:

1. Rivalry intensity
2. Buyer bargaining power
3. Supplier bargaining power
4. Threat of substitutes
5. Threat of new entrants

Then classify current performance as:
- Structural edge
- Cyclical tailwind
- Mixed

Do not confuse cyclical margin expansion with durable moat.

## 3) Moat Source Scoring

Score each source `0-5` using explicit evidence.

| Moat source | Typical evidence | Common erosion path |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Cost advantage | Lower delivered cost, scale procurement, superior process | Commodity input shock, technology catch-up |
| Switching costs | Workflow lock-in, integration depth, retraining burden | Open standards, interoperability tools |
| Network effects | Value rises with users/data density | Multi-homing, niche fragmentation |
| Intangible assets | Brand trust, patents, licenses, distribution rights | Regulation changes, brand dilution |
| Efficient scale | Small market supports few profitable players | Market expansion inviting entrants |
| Process/data advantage | Proprietary data loops, operational know-how | Data parity, automation commoditization |

Suggested weighting:
- Cost advantage: 20%
- Switching costs: 20%
- Network effects: 20%
- Intangible assets: 15%
- Efficient scale: 10%
- Process/data advantage: 15%

Weighted moat score:
`sum(source_score * weight)` where each source score is 0-5.

Durability bands:
- `>=4.0`: strong moat, likely durable
- `3.0-3.9`: defendable but monitorable
- `<3.0`: weak or unproven moat

## 4) Management and Capital Allocation

Judge management on actions, not narrative:

1. Reinvestment discipline: Does incremental capital earn > cost of capital?
2. Buyback discipline: Are buybacks counter-cyclical and valuation-aware?
3. M&A quality: Is deal logic coherent and post-merger ROIC credible?
4. Balance sheet posture: Is leverage aligned with business volatility?
5. Governance quality: Is compensation linked to value creation metrics?

Red flags:
- EPS growth driven mainly by buybacks with weak FCF growth
- Serial acquisitions masking organic deceleration
- Repeated "adjusted" earnings exclusions with no cleanup trend
- Incentives tied to short-term revenue, not returns

## 5) Required Output Block

When finished, produce:

1. Business model summary (5-8 lines)
2. Moat score table with evidence
3. Durability verdict (`Strong / Medium / Weak`)
4. Top 3 moat break risks
5. Confidence score (`High / Medium / Low`) with reasons
