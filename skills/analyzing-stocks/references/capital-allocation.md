# Capital Allocation

## Objective

Judge whether management converts operating success into per-share value creation.

## Core Questions

1. Is reinvestment earning above the cost of capital?
2. Are buybacks valuation-aware or merely anti-dilution?
3. Is dividend policy aligned with business maturity and balance-sheet needs?
4. Has M&A created or destroyed value?
5. Is leverage appropriate for the volatility of the business?

## Review Areas

### Reinvestment

- Compare incremental ROIC with headline ROIC
- Check whether capex and R&D are improving future economics
- Flag growth spending that does not improve unit economics

### Shareholder Returns

- Separate true buybacks from SBC offset
- Check payout ratio against cycle and liquidity needs
- Flag aggressive shareholder return programs funded by debt

### M&A

- Evaluate strategic fit, price paid, and post-deal margin/ROIC outcomes
- Flag serial acquisitions masking weak organic performance

### Dilution

- Track diluted share count over time
- Highlight SBC, convertibles, warrants, and recurring equity issuance

## Quality Bands

- `Strong`: capital consistently compounds per-share value
- `Mixed`: some good decisions but material leakages remain
- `Weak`: capital deployment destroys value or hides weak economics

## Required Output Block

Include:
1. Capital allocation verdict
2. Reinvestment quality
3. Buyback/dividend assessment
4. M&A assessment
5. Dilution risk summary
