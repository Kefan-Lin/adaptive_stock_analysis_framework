# Analyzing Software Platforms

## Overview

Provide industry-specific logic for software and platform businesses.
Use as a companion to `$analyzing-stocks`, not as a standalone replacement for the controller.

## Controller Contract

- Return only:
  1. subtype
  2. KPI tree
  3. accounting traps and normalizations
  4. valuation routing
  5. risk checklist
  6. monitoring triggers
- Do not restate generic moat, evidence-ledger, or report-template guidance.

## 1. Industry Router

Choose one subtype:
- Mission-critical infrastructure: databases, security, observability, workflow backbone
- Vertical SaaS: workflow software tied to one vertical
- Data/platform software: usage-led, API-led, or data-network businesses
- Commerce/platform software: payments, POS, merchant ecosystem, app marketplace

State:
- why this subtype fits
- 3 key value drivers
- 3 failure triggers

## 2. KPI Tree

- Demand: ARR, bookings, new logo adds, NRR, gross retention
- Pricing: seat expansion, usage growth, price uplift, attach rate
- Cost structure: gross margin, sales efficiency, R&D leverage
- Cash economics: FCF margin, OCF conversion, CAC payback, LTV/CAC
- Monetization shift: pricing model migration, usage-based adoption, AI monetization,
  platform attach, bundle penetration, and contract-duration changes
- Per-share leakage: SBC, dilution, acquisition roll-up dependence

## 3. Accounting Traps and Normalizations

- SBC can overstate true FCF quality; always examine per-share FCF.
- Deferred revenue and billings can make growth look healthier than demand.
- Capitalized commissions and implementation costs can flatter margins.
- Marketplace or payments names may require principal-vs-agent normalization.
- Acquired growth can hide weakening organic expansion.
- Usage-based or AI monetization can raise TAM and revenue volatility at the same time;
  separate real consumption durability from temporary overage or hype-driven workloads.

## 4. Valuation Routing

- Primary: FCFF DCF
- Secondary: EV/EBIT or P/E for mature names; EV/Sales only with explicit margin bridge
- Critical assumptions: NRR durability, steady-state margin, SBC normalization, FCF conversion, pricing model migration, and multiple re-rating from improved platform durability
- Avoid: revenue-only valuation without margin path or dilution discussion
- If a product shift changes retention, usage intensity, or platform breadth, compare old
  seat/subscription economics with the new usage or AI monetization regime before changing Base multiples.

## 5. Risk Checklist

- Open-source or AI commoditization
- Platform displacement or bundling pressure
- Weakening seat expansion or usage intensity
- SBC-driven dilution overwhelming operating progress
- Customer concentration or vendor consolidation

## 6. Deliverables Back to Controller

- Subtype and why it fits
- KPI tree with 4-6 metrics
- Key accounting normalizations
- Primary and secondary valuation methods
- Top 5 risks and 3 monitor triggers
